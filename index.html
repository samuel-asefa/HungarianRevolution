Hungarian Revolution (1956)

Cold War Infographic

Historical Context

After World War 2, the Soviet Union took control of Hungary and made it a communist satellite state. Hungary was ruled by a harsh Stalinist leader named Mátyás Rákosi. The secret police (called the ÁVH) arrested and tortured a lot of people. Hungarians were really unhappy.

When Stalin died in 1953 things started to slowly change. In 1956 Khrushchev gave a speech criticizing Stalin which gave people hope that maybe the USSR was changing.

Event Details

On October 23 1956 students and workers marched in Budapest demanding freedom and for Soviet troops to leave. The secret police shot at them which made things way worse. People started fighting back and it turned into a full revolution.

Imre Nagy became the new prime minister and said Hungary would leave the Warsaw Pact. That was a big deal because it meant Hungary wanted to be neutral and not under Soviet control anymore.

The US had been encouraging eastern europeans to resist communism through Radio Free Europe but when the revolution actually happened they didn't send any help which was kind of a betrayal.

Outcome

On November 4 the USSR sent over 1000 tanks into Budapest and crushed the revolution. About 2500-3000 Hungarians died. 200,000 people fled to the west as refugees. Imre Nagy was arrested and later executed in 1958.

This was a win for Communism in the short term but it made the USSR look really bad to the rest of the world.
